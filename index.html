Scott Cheng-Hsin Yang

I am currently a research associate (post-doc) with Prof. Daniel Wolpert in the Computational and Biological Learning Lab at University of Cambridge. I study Bayesian active sensing in sensorimotor control. In particular, we go beyond the conventional active sensing task of visual search and explore both experimentally and theoretically active sensing in more complex scenarios.

I did my PhD with Prof. John Bechhoefer in the Physics Department at Simon Fraser University. I modelled the DNA replication program in Eukaryotes as a stochastic nucleation-and-growth process and applied the theory to analyze data from several types of replication experiment.
